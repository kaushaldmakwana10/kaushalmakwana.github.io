Research Direction

The question that
needs a PhD to answer

Industry practice revealed the problem. Doctoral research is how I intend to solve it.

How do we build explainable AI frameworks for manufacturing quality control that are not just accurate, but interpretable enough that engineers on the factory floor actually trust and act on them?

At a major truck manufacturing facility, I built a predictive model with 90% accuracy. Operators consistently ignored it — not because it was wrong, but because it could not explain itself in terms they could engage with critically. That gap between prediction and adoption is the human-AI decision gap. It shows up everywhere AI meets experienced operators. Closing it requires doctoral-level research into explainability, real-time decision support, and human-AI collaboration in industrial systems.

When the Model Knows but the Manager Has to Decide: A Simulation-Optimization Framework for Real-Time Manufacturing Resource Planning
TECHNICAL NOTE · ZENODO · 10.5281/zenodo.20848015 · 2026

Documents a three-layer simulation-optimization framework for inspection backlog management. Layer 1 is a linear programming model solving for optimal resource allocation. Layer 2 extends the solution into a 30-day Monte Carlo probabilistic forecast. Layer 3 integrates OLS regression validated on 4,028 production records at 90% accuracy. Includes a four-phase AI integration roadmap from current decision support to autonomous manufacturing intelligence.
